AI & Technology
3 deep-dive stories
The most significant AI and tech developments of the day — model releases, research breakthroughs, product launches, and infrastructure shifts. Each story gets the full treatment: concept explanation, diagram, public opinion, insights, and a deep dive.
🔐
Cybersecurity
3 deep-dive stories

03   Inside each story
A complete guide to every section
Each AI and Cybersecurity story is structured as a layered deep-dive. Click a story headline to expand it and find these sections inside.
⚡
TL;DR
Always visible
A single sentence that tells you the most important thing about the story before you open it. If that's all you have time for, this is it.
📌
Why It Matters
First section inside
The practical stakes — who is affected, what changes, and why someone working in tech or security should care. Skips the hype and goes straight to consequence.
🧠
Concept
4-paragraph explainer
A structured explanation of the core technology or idea behind the story. Paragraph 1 gives a real-world analogy. Paragraph 2 explains how it technically works. Paragraph 3 ties it to this specific story. Paragraph 4 covers broader implications.
📊
Visual Diagram
SVG illustration
An SVG diagram generated by Claude to illustrate the concept — attack chains, data flows, architecture maps, timelines, or comparisons. Chosen based on what best communicates the structure of the idea.
👥
Public Opinion
Community reactions
What Hacker News, Reddit (r/technology, r/netsec), and Security Twitter are actually saying about this story. Each community gets its own collapsible entry — click to read the full sentiment.
📊 Sentiment Summary
A 2-3 sentence synthesis of the dominant collective mood across all communities — the shared concern, excitement, or skepticism.
😈 Devil's Advocate
A sharp counter-perspective that challenges the dominant public sentiment — an overlooked irony, an inconvenient truth, or a reframe that makes you reconsider the story.
💡
Insights
3 expandable cards
Three perspective cards that add conceptual depth to the story. Each is written through a distinct lens — click any card to reveal the insight and its explanation.
🔬 Scientific — how it works 📜 Historical — what it echoes 🌍 Societal — who it affects
💭
Deep Dive
Closing narrative
The richest section of each story — a layered conclusion that synthesizes everything covered and leaves you with something to think about.
💭 Narrative
A riveting 3-4 sentence thread that weaves together the concept, the event, and the stakes — written like the opening of a great longform piece.
🎯 How This Affects You
Specific and personal — how this story connects to your day-to-day work, your tools, your security posture, or your career.
🔭 Outlook
A forward-looking conclusion: what this story likely accelerates or disrupts, and what to watch for in the coming weeks or months.
🏷️
Tech Tags
Contextual tooltips
Up to 3 specific technologies, protocols, or CVE IDs that appear in the story. Each tag includes a description of what it is and why it's relevant here — hover or tap to read. Never generic terms.
04   Notables format
The lighter-format cards
Notable cards cover broader stories from policy, business, research, and society. Lighter than the deep-dive format — designed for a quick read.
📰
Summary

💡
Why This Applies to You
How this story could matter to someone in tech or security — career implications, tools to watch, policy awareness, or market shifts.
05   Card Maker
Turn any article into a story card
Card Maker lets you generate a full deep-dive card — concept, diagram, public opinion, insights, and deep dive — from any article URL or pasted text. It uses the same AI pipeline as the daily digest, on demand.
✨ Card Maker
1
Open Card Maker
Click the Card Maker button in the digest header. It slides open as a panel below the navigation tabs.
2
Paste a URL or article text
Drop in any article URL — Card Maker will fetch the page content via a secure proxy. If the site blocks fetching, paste the article text directly into the text box instead.
3
Choose a category and hit Generate
Select AI & Technology, Cybersecurity, or Notable to match the article's domain. Then click Generate Card — Claude analyzes the content and builds a complete story card.
4
Read your card
A fully formatted card appears — identical structure to the daily digest cards. Expand the collapsible sections (Concept, Visual Diagram, Devil's Advocate, How This Affects You, Outlook) to explore the full analysis.
5
Generate more cards
Cards are added to the session view so you can compare multiple articles side by side. Use the reset button to clear the panel and start fresh.
Weekly limit: Card Maker is rate-limited to 10 cards per week to keep the API costs manageable. The limit resets every Sunday. If you hit the limit, the button will show how many days remain.
URL not loading? Some sites block automated fetching. If the URL fails, copy the article text from your browser and paste it directly into the text box — the card will generate from the pasted content instead.
✅
Works great for
Long-form tech or security articles, research papers, in-depth blog posts, and news stories with enough substance for meaningful analysis. The more context in the article, the richer the card.
⚠️
Less effective for
Very short news briefs, paywalled articles (where only the preview loads), social media posts, or press releases with minimal technical detail. Pasting full text directly usually helps in these cases.
