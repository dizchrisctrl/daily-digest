⚠ Vulnerability Advisory

WhatsApp Metadata Leak Lets Strangers Profile Users Without Sending a Message

Medium ⚠ No Fix Yet

Security researchers have discovered that WhatsApp exposes sufficient user metadata — including online/offline status, profile photo visibility, last-seen timestamps, and group membership signals — to allow strangers to build behavioral profiles of any user without ever sending them a message. With over two billion users worldwide, this finding highlights that end-to-end encryption of message content does not protect the surrounding metadata, which can be exploited for surveillance, stalking, social engineering, and targeted attacks.

📅 Apr 20, 2026 10:33 AM EDT| Source: darkreading ↗
▶Attack Flow
WhatsApp Metadata Leak — Stranger Profiling via Exposed Status and Presence APIs
An attacker who knows a target's phone number can systematically poll WhatsApp's presence and status APIs to observe online/offline patterns, profile photo availability, and other exposed metadata signals. By correlating these data points over time, the attacker can infer daily schedules, sleep patterns, and behavioral routines — all without sending a single message or appearing in the target's contact list.

❓Am I Affected?

You are likely at risk if any of the following apply:

⚠You or your organization's employees use WhatsApp for personal or business communication.
⚠Your WhatsApp privacy settings for 'Last Seen,' 'Online,' and 'Profile Photo' are set to 'Everyone' (the default).
⚠You are a journalist, activist, executive, or other individual who may be specifically targeted for surveillance or social engineering.
⚠Your organization relies on WhatsApp's end-to-end encryption as its sole privacy assurance for sensitive communications.
✓You have already restricted all WhatsApp privacy settings (Last Seen, Online, Profile Photo, About) to 'My Contacts' or 'Nobody' and are aware of residual metadata exposure.
✓You do not use WhatsApp and your organization does not permit its use for business communications.
🛠Fix & Recommendations
⚠ Immediate 24–72 hours
  • 1Open WhatsApp → Settings → Privacy → Last Seen & Online → set 'Last Seen' to 'Nobody' and toggle 'Who can see when I'm online' to 'Same as Last Seen' (if available) or 'My Contacts.'
  • 2In the same Privacy menu, set 'Profile Photo' visibility to 'My Contacts' or 'Nobody' to reduce exposure to unknown parties.
  • 3Set 'About' information visibility to 'My Contacts' or 'Nobody' to limit additional metadata leakage.
  • 4Advise high-risk personnel (executives, security staff, journalists) to audit their WhatsApp privacy settings immediately and consider migrating sensitive discussions to Signal, which implements Sealed Sender metadata protections.
  • 5Issue an internal awareness bulletin explaining that end-to-end encryption does not protect metadata, and that presence/status signals can be observed by strangers who know a user's phone number.
● Strategic This week & beyond
  • 1Evaluate and adopt messaging platforms with metadata-minimizing architectures (e.g., Signal with Sealed Sender) for sensitive organizational communications rather than relying solely on WhatsApp's content encryption.
  • 2Develop and enforce a corporate mobile messaging policy that specifies approved platforms and mandates privacy-hardened settings for any permitted consumer messaging apps.
  • 3Engage with Meta/WhatsApp through your vendor relationship or industry groups to advocate for server-side metadata minimization, such as restricting presence API queries to mutual contacts only.
  • 4Incorporate metadata exposure risk into your organization's threat modeling process — treat behavioral pattern leakage as a reconnaissance vector alongside traditional data exfiltration scenarios.
  • 5For high-value targets, implement operational security (OPSEC) training that covers metadata hygiene across all communication platforms, not just message content protection.
📚Concept BriefingClick a tag to expand
Metadata Leakage
The exposure of structured information surrounding a communication (who, when, how often) rather than the communication's content itself. Over time, metadata can reveal relationships, habits, and routines.
This is the core vulnerability — WhatsApp's exposed presence and status metadata allows strangers to profile users even though message content remains encrypted.
End-to-End Encryption
A security method where only the communicating users can read the messages, with the service provider unable to decrypt the content in transit or at rest.
WhatsApp uses E2EE for message content, but this research shows encryption alone does not protect the metadata envelope, creating a false sense of total privacy.
Sealed Sender Protocol
A metadata protection technique, used by Signal, that hides the sender's identity from the messaging server during delivery, reducing the server's ability to log communication patterns.
Cited as the architectural alternative that WhatsApp has not adopted — it represents the class of metadata-minimizing design that could mitigate this exposure.
Presence Oracle Attack
A technique where an attacker repeatedly queries a service's online/offline or availability status for a target to infer behavioral patterns without direct interaction.
The researchers demonstrated this exact pattern — polling WhatsApp's presence APIs to determine when a target is active, asleep, or away, building a behavioral profile over time.
Social Engineering Enablement
The use of gathered personal or behavioral information to craft convincing pretexts for manipulating a target into taking a desired action, such as clicking a link or divulging credentials.
Behavioral profiles derived from WhatsApp metadata (active hours, routines) directly enable more effective spear-phishing and impersonation attacks against targets.
🔍Threat Hunting Signals
Medium Anomalous high-frequency API queries against WhatsApp presence endpoints ►
An attacker profiling a target would generate unusually frequent polling of online/offline status from a single source. While individual users cannot observe this server-side, organizations using WhatsApp Business API or network monitoring may detect abnormal query volumes or patterns in API traffic logs targeting specific user accounts.
WhatsApp Business API logsNetwork proxy/firewall logs (outbound to WhatsApp API endpoints)Mobile Device Management (MDM) network telemetry
Medium Reconnaissance correlation across platforms ►
Attackers profiling via WhatsApp metadata may cross-reference findings with other platforms (LinkedIn, social media). Look for correlated spikes in profile views or connection requests to individuals whose WhatsApp numbers may have been targeted, particularly for high-value personnel.
LinkedIn admin/analytics dashboardsCorporate social media monitoring toolsEmail gateway logs (for subsequent phishing attempts)
High Targeted social engineering following metadata-derived scheduling ►
Phishing or vishing attempts that arrive at times precisely matching a target's known active hours (as inferred from presence monitoring) may indicate prior metadata reconnaissance. Correlate timestamps of incoming social engineering attempts with the target's typical WhatsApp online patterns.
