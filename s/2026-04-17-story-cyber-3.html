ZionSiphon: Purpose-Built Malware Targets Water Treatment Infrastructure

Critical ⚠ No Fix Yet

A newly discovered OT-specific malware called ZionSiphon has been identified targeting water treatment and desalination facilities with the explicit goal of sabotaging physical operations. The malware manipulates industrial control systems — including PLCs, SCADA interfaces, and protocols like Modbus and DNP3 — to alter chemical dosing and purification processes while spoofing operator displays to hide the attack. This represents a dangerous evolution in ICS malware, joining the lineage of Stuxnet, Industroyer, and Triton but shifting focus to water infrastructure, one of the most critical and under-resourced sectors in cybersecurity.

❓Am I Affected?

You are likely at risk if any of the following apply:

⚠You operate or manage a water treatment or desalination facility with SCADA/ICS systems
⚠Your environment uses Modbus or DNP3 protocols for process control in water infrastructure
⚠Your OT network is not fully air-gapped or has IT/OT convergence points with internet-facing components
⚠Your water treatment PLCs and HMIs run on legacy systems without recent firmware validation
✓You operate only IT environments with no OT/ICS infrastructure connected to physical processes
✓Your OT environment is fully air-gapped with no remote access, verified network segmentation, and continuous integrity monitoring on all PLCs
🛠Fix & Recommendations
⚠ Immediate 24–72 hours
  • 1Immediately audit all remote access paths into OT/ICS networks at water treatment facilities — disable any unnecessary VPN, RDP, or vendor remote access connections to SCADA systems.
  • 2Verify PLC firmware integrity by comparing current PLC program checksums against known-good baselines; flag any unauthorized changes to dosing logic or setpoint parameters.
  • 3Enable and review logging on all Modbus/DNP3 traffic using OT-aware network monitoring tools (e.g., Claroty, Dragos, Nozomi Networks) and look for anomalous command patterns or unexpected writes to PLCs.
  • 4Cross-validate HMI sensor readings against independent physical measurements (manual water quality sampling, inline analyzers with separate data paths) to detect spoofed readings.
  • 5Ensure safety interlocks and emergency shutdown systems are functioning independently and have not been tampered with; test manual overrides.
● Strategic This week & beyond
  • 1Implement robust IT/OT network segmentation with unidirectional security gateways (data diodes) between corporate IT and process control networks.
  • 2Deploy continuous OT network monitoring and anomaly detection platforms capable of deep packet inspection of Modbus, DNP3, and proprietary SCADA protocols.
  • 3Establish a PLC configuration management program with regular integrity checks, version-controlled backups, and automated alerts on unauthorized logic changes.
  • 4Develop and regularly exercise incident response plans specific to OT/ICS compromise scenarios, including manual operational fallback procedures for water treatment.
  • 5Engage with CISA, WaterISAC, and sector-specific information sharing organizations to stay current on ZionSiphon indicators and collaborate on sector-wide defensive measures.
📚Concept BriefingClick a tag to expand
OT/ICS Malware
Malware specifically designed to target Operational Technology and Industrial Control Systems that manage physical processes like water treatment, power generation, and manufacturing.
ZionSiphon is purpose-built OT malware targeting water treatment ICS environments, joining an elite class of kinetic-effect malware like Stuxnet and Triton.
SCADA Systems
Supervisory Control and Data Acquisition systems that provide centralized monitoring and control over distributed industrial processes via sensors, PLCs, and human-machine interfaces.
ZionSiphon targets SCADA components at water facilities — manipulating PLCs and spoofing HMI displays to hide sabotage from operators.
Industrial Protocol Abuse
Exploitation of specialized communication protocols like Modbus and DNP3 that were designed for reliability in isolated networks and often lack authentication or encryption.
ZionSiphon leverages inherent weaknesses in Modbus/DNP3 to send unauthorized commands that alter chemical dosing without proper authentication barriers.
Sensor Spoofing
The act of sending falsified data to monitoring displays so operators believe a process is running normally when it has actually been compromised.
A core capability of ZionSiphon is spoofing HMI sensor readings to mask dangerous changes to water treatment parameters.
Critical Infrastructure Targeting
Cyberattacks aimed at essential services like water, energy, and transportation that can cause physical harm, public health emergencies, or societal disruption.
ZionSiphon explicitly targets water treatment — a sector historically under-resourced in cybersecurity — with potential to endanger public health at scale.
🔍Threat Hunting Signals
High Anomalous Modbus/DNP3 write commands to PLCs ►
Look for unexpected write commands (Modbus function codes 05, 06, 15, 16) or DNP3 direct operate commands targeting chemical dosing setpoints or safety interlock registers, especially from unusual source IPs or during non-maintenance windows. These may indicate unauthorized PLC reprogramming by ZionSiphon.
OT network traffic captures (PCAP)Modbus/DNP3 deep packet inspection logs (Claroty, Dragos, Nozomi Networks)Firewall logs at IT/OT boundary
High PLC logic or firmware integrity deviation ►
Compare current PLC program checksums and configuration against known-good baselines. Any unauthorized deviation in dosing logic, setpoint values, or safety interlock configurations may indicate ZionSiphon has modified controller behavior.
PLC configuration management toolsAsset inventory and change management systemsEngineering workstation audit logs
High Discrepancy between HMI readings and physical sensors ►
Cross-reference values displayed on SCADA HMIs with independent physical measurements (manual sampling, inline analyzers on separate data paths). Persistent discrepancies — especially in chlorine levels, pH, turbidity — may indicate sensor spoofing by ZionSiphon.
SCADA historian databasesIndependent inline water quality analyzer logsManual sampling records
Medium Unexpected outbound connections from OT network segments ►
Monitor for any network connections originating from OT/ICS segments toward external IP addresses or unusual internal hosts. OT environments should have minimal outbound traffic; any anomaly could indicate C2 communication or data exfiltration by the malware.
OT network monitoring platformsFirewall and router logs at OT perimeterDNS query logs from OT segments
Medium Unauthorized access to engineering workstations ►
Review authentication logs on engineering workstations and SCADA servers for unusual login times, failed authentication attempts, or use of service/default credentials that could indicate an attacker gaining access to reprogram PLCs.
