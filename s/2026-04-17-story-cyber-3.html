ZionSiphon: Purpose-Built Malware Targets Water Treatment and Desalination Systems

Critical ⚠ Mitigation Only

ZionSiphon is a newly discovered malware framework purpose-built to infiltrate and sabotage operational technology (OT) in water treatment and desalination plants. It targets SCADA/ICS components—including PLCs, HMIs, and industrial protocols like Modbus and DNP3—to send rogue commands that can alter chemical dosing, valve positions, and safety interlocks. This represents a significant escalation in OT-targeted threats, directly endangering public health and water supply safety for millions of people.

❓Am I Affected?

You are likely at risk if any of the following apply:

⚠You operate or manage a water treatment or desalination facility with networked SCADA/ICS systems
⚠Your environment uses Modbus, DNP3, or OPC UA protocols for process control in water infrastructure
⚠Your OT network has any connectivity to corporate IT networks or the internet (directly or via VPN)
✓You rely solely on air-gapped OT networks with no remote access, USB policies enforced, and verified network segmentation
✓Your environment has no water treatment or desalination OT systems (e.g., you are a purely IT-based organization)
⚠Your water treatment PLCs lack authentication for command execution and firmware updates
🛠Fix & Recommendations
⚠ Immediate 24–72 hours
  • 1Immediately audit and disable all unnecessary remote access paths to OT networks—shut down unused VPN tunnels, RDP sessions, and internet-exposed HMI/SCADA portals within 24 hours.
  • 2Deploy or verify network segmentation between IT and OT zones using industrial firewalls or demilitarized zones (DMZs); block all Modbus (TCP/502), DNP3 (TCP/20000), and OPC UA (TCP/4840) traffic from crossing IT/OT boundaries.
  • 3Enable deep packet inspection (DPI) on any remaining IT-OT conduits to detect anomalous industrial protocol commands; configure alerts for unexpected write commands or configuration changes to PLCs.
  • 4Verify that all PLC programs and HMI configurations match known-good baselines by comparing current logic snapshots against offline backups; flag any unauthorized changes for immediate investigation.
  • 5Coordinate with CISA, WaterISAC, and your SCADA/PLC vendors to obtain the latest threat intelligence and any available detection signatures specific to ZionSiphon.
● Strategic This week & beyond
  • 1Implement a Purdue Model (ISA/IEC 62443) network architecture with enforced zone segmentation, data diodes for one-way telemetry flows, and strict conduit policies between OT levels.
  • 2Deploy an OT-specific intrusion detection system (e.g., Claroty, Dragos, Nozomi Networks) capable of parsing Modbus/DNP3/OPC UA traffic and alerting on anomalous command sequences or unauthorized PLC reprogramming.
  • 3Establish a PLC configuration management program with version-controlled logic backups, scheduled integrity checks, and change-management workflows that require dual authorization for any control logic modifications.
  • 4Conduct tabletop exercises and red team assessments specifically simulating OT-targeted malware scenarios in water treatment environments to validate incident response plans and operator awareness.
  • 5Advocate for and pursue dedicated cybersecurity funding for water utility OT environments, including hiring or contracting OT security specialists, as the sector has historically been underfunded relative to the threat level.
📚Concept BriefingClick a tag to expand
OT-Specific Malware
Malware purpose-built to interact with industrial control systems (PLCs, SCADA, HMIs) using specialized protocols, aiming to manipulate physical processes rather than steal data.
ZionSiphon is a textbook example—designed from the ground up to speak Modbus/DNP3 and target the exact process logic found in water treatment and desalination plants.
SCADA / ICS
Supervisory Control and Data Acquisition (SCADA) and Industrial Control Systems (ICS) are the computerized systems that monitor and control physical infrastructure like water plants, power grids, and factories.
These are the direct targets of ZionSiphon; compromising them allows an attacker to alter chemical dosing, valve states, and safety systems in a water facility.
Industrial Protocol Abuse
Legacy industrial protocols like Modbus and DNP3 were designed for reliability, not security—they typically lack authentication, allowing anyone with network access to send commands.
ZionSiphon exploits this design weakness to send unauthorized control commands that PLCs accept as legitimate, enabling process manipulation without credentials.
Safety Interlock Bypass
Safety interlocks are automated safeguards that shut down or restrict operations when dangerous conditions are detected (e.g., excessive chemical levels or overpressure).
ZionSiphon may disable these protections to allow harmful process states to persist undetected, dramatically increasing the risk of physical harm.
Water Sector Resilience
The water sector encompasses drinking water treatment, wastewater, and desalination—critical services that are often underfunded and understaffed for cybersecurity compared to energy or finance.
ZionSiphon's emergence highlights the acute vulnerability of water utilities, which now face nation-state-caliber threats with limited defensive resources.
🔍Threat Hunting Signals
High Anomalous Modbus/DNP3 write commands to PLCs ►
Look for unexpected write-multiple-registers (Modbus function code 0x10) or DNP3 direct-operate commands targeting chemical dosing or valve control points outside of scheduled maintenance windows. These may indicate ZionSiphon issuing rogue commands to alter process parameters.

High PLC logic or firmware changes without change tickets ►
Monitor for PLC program uploads, downloads, or firmware flashes that do not correspond to approved change-management records. ZionSiphon may reprogram PLCs to alter process control logic. Compare current PLC logic hashes against known-good baselines.

High Sensor reading discrepancies between independent sources ►
Cross-reference SCADA-reported sensor values (chlorine levels, pH, pressure, flow rates) against independent physical instruments or lab samples. Persistent discrepancies may indicate ZionSiphon is spoofing sensor telemetry to mask process manipulation.
SCADA historian databasesIndependent lab/field measurement recordsProcess alarm logs
High Unexpected IT-to-OT lateral movement traffic ►
Alert on any new connections from IT-zone hosts to OT-zone IP ranges, especially targeting TCP ports 502 (Modbus), 20000 (DNP3), or 4840 (OPC UA). This may indicate the initial stage of ZionSiphon pivoting from a compromised IT asset into the OT network.

High Safety system alarm suppression or interlock disabling ►
Watch for safety instrumented system (SIS) alarms being acknowledged programmatically, interlocks being placed in bypass mode, or safety controller mode changes occurring outside maintenance windows. ZionSiphon may target these to allow dangerous conditions to persist.
