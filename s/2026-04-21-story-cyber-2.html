Scattered Spider's 'Tylerb' Pleads Guilty to Wire Fraud and Identity Theft

High ⚠ Mitigation Only

Tyler Buchanan, a 24-year-old British member of the Scattered Spider cybercrime collective operating under the handle "Tylerb," has pleaded guilty to wire fraud conspiracy and aggravated identity theft. He helped breach at least a dozen major technology companies and steal tens of millions of dollars in cryptocurrency using SMS phishing (smishing) campaigns that harvested employee credentials and MFA tokens in real time. The case represents one of the most significant prosecutions of a Western cybercrime collective and underscores the persistent danger of social engineering attacks against organizations relying on TOTP-based multi-factor authentication.

▶Attack Flow
Scattered Spider — "Tylerb" Pleads Guilty to Wire Fraud and Identity Theft
Scattered Spider sent carefully crafted SMS phishing messages impersonating corporate IT departments and SSO login portals to employees of targeted tech companies. Victims were directed to pixel-perfect replicas of legitimate login pages that captured credentials and MFA tokens in real time (adversary-in-the-middle phishing), allowing attackers to hijack sessions before tokens expired and pivot through internal systems to cryptocurrency wallets and sensitive data.

❓Am I Affected?

You are likely at risk if any of the following apply:

⚠Your organization uses TOTP-based (time-based one-time password) or SMS-based multi-factor authentication for employee SSO logins
⚠Your employees receive corporate IT notifications or password reset links via SMS or email that could be impersonated
⚠Your organization holds or manages cryptocurrency assets or has access to high-value digital wallets
⚠Your environment lacks real-time anomaly detection for unusual login locations, impossible travel, or new device enrollments
✓Your organization has fully deployed FIDO2/WebAuthn hardware security keys or passkeys as the sole MFA method for all employees
✓Your environment enforces certificate-based or device-bound authentication that cannot be proxied through adversary-in-the-middle infrastructure
🛠Fix & Recommendations
⚠ Immediate 24–72 hours
  • 1Audit all MFA methods in use across your identity provider (Okta, Azure AD, Google Workspace, etc.) — identify any accounts still relying on TOTP, SMS OTP, or push-notification MFA and flag them for immediate upgrade.
  • 2Enable conditional access policies that block authentication from unrecognized devices, anomalous geolocations, or non-compliant endpoints (e.g., Azure AD Conditional Access, Okta Sign-On Policies).
  • 3Issue an internal security awareness alert warning employees about SMS phishing campaigns impersonating IT helpdesks and SSO portals; instruct staff to verify any credential-reset requests through a known internal channel, never via SMS links.
  • 4Review identity provider logs for the past 30 days for signs of session hijacking: impossible travel events, new MFA device enrollments from unusual IPs, or rapid sequential logins from disparate geographies.
  • 5Restrict or disable self-service MFA enrollment and password reset flows temporarily if you suspect active targeting, requiring helpdesk-verified identity before any credential changes.
● Strategic This week & beyond
  • 1Mandate FIDO2/WebAuthn phishing-resistant MFA (hardware security keys or platform passkeys) for all employees, prioritizing privileged accounts, IT staff, and personnel with access to financial systems or cryptocurrency infrastructure.
  • 2Deploy an adversary-in-the-middle-resistant SSO architecture by enforcing device-bound certificates or managed device attestation as a prerequisite for authentication, ensuring stolen credentials alone cannot establish a session.
  • 3Implement a robust phishing simulation and security awareness program that specifically includes SMS phishing (smishing) scenarios, with measurable reporting metrics and escalation procedures.
  • 4Establish continuous identity threat detection and response (ITDR) capabilities that monitor for anomalous authentication patterns, session token reuse from new IPs, and bulk credential harvesting indicators in real time.
  • 5Develop and rehearse an incident response playbook specifically for social engineering and credential theft scenarios, including rapid token revocation, forced re-authentication, and coordinated communications to affected employees.
📚Concept BriefingClick a tag to expand
SMS Phishing (Smishing)
A social engineering attack that uses text messages to trick victims into clicking malicious links or revealing credentials, exploiting trust in mobile communications.
Scattered Spider's entire initial access strategy relied on mass SMS phishing campaigns impersonating corporate IT departments to harvest employee login credentials.
Adversary-in-the-Middle Phishing
A real-time phishing technique where the attacker's infrastructure sits between the victim and the legitimate server, relaying credentials and session tokens to hijack authenticated sessions.
This technique allowed Scattered Spider to defeat TOTP-based MFA by capturing and replaying one-time codes before they expired.
FIDO2/WebAuthn
A phishing-resistant authentication standard using public-key cryptography tied to the legitimate website's domain, making credential theft via fake login pages mathematically impossible.
FIDO2 adoption is the primary technical countermeasure that would have prevented Scattered Spider's credential harvesting attacks against targeted tech companies.
Social Engineering
Manipulating people into performing actions or divulging confidential information by exploiting human psychology rather than technical vulnerabilities.
Scattered Spider's success demonstrated that social engineering remains the most cost-effective attack vector, bypassing billions of dollars in technical security controls by targeting the human layer.
Loosely Organized Cybercrime Collectives
Decentralized groups of cybercriminals who collaborate informally across jurisdictions, often communicating via Telegram or Discord, without a rigid hierarchical structure.
Scattered Spider's distributed, informal structure made attribution and prosecution difficult, making Buchanan's guilty plea a landmark achievement for cross-border law enforcement cooperation.
🔍Threat Hunting Signals
High Anomalous SSO login from new device or geolocation immediately after MFA token issuance ►
Look for authentication events where a user's MFA token is entered and a session is established from an IP address or device fingerprint that differs from the user's typical profile, especially within seconds of the MFA challenge — this indicates real-time AitM credential relay.
