CISA's KEV Clock Starts Ticking on Four Actively Exploited Bugs

CVSS
9.9
Critical ✓ Patch Available

CISA added four actively exploited CVEs to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities (KEV) catalog on April 25, 2026, covering SimpleHelp remote support software, Samsung MagicINFO 9 Server, and D-Link DIR-823X routers. Federal civilian agencies must remediate by a May 2026 deadline under BOD 22-01, and private-sector defenders should treat the listing as a de facto patch-now signal because exploitation is already occurring in the wild.

📅 Apr 25, 2026 1:08 AM EDT| Source: The Hacker News ↗ |CVE-2024-57726
▶Attack Flow
CISA KEV Update: SimpleHelp, Samsung MagicINFO 9, D-Link DIR-823X Actively Exploited
The flagship issue, CVE-2024-57726 in SimpleHelp, is a missing-authorization flaw (CVSS 9.9) that lets unauthenticated or low-privilege actors reach administrative functionality, enabling downstream pivot into MSP-managed customer environments. The MagicINFO 9 Server and D-Link DIR-823X bugs similarly expose internet- or LAN-reachable management surfaces that adversaries are leveraging for initial access and persistence on poorly inventoried edge devices.

❓Am I Affected?

You are likely at risk if any of the following apply:

⚠Your environment runs a SimpleHelp server (self-hosted or MSP-hosted) reachable from the internet
⚠You operate Samsung MagicINFO 9 Server for digital signage in corporate, retail, or transit settings
⚠Your environment includes D-Link DIR-823X routers at branch, home-office, or small-business edges
⚠You are a managed service provider whose technicians use SimpleHelp to access client endpoints
✓Your environment uses none of these products and has confirmed this via asset inventory
⚠You are a U.S. federal civilian executive branch agency subject to BOD 22-01
🛠Fix & Recommendations
⚠ Immediate 24–72 hours
  • 1Inventory all SimpleHelp servers, Samsung MagicINFO 9 Server instances, and D-Link DIR-823X routers across owned and MSP-managed environments within 48 hours.
  • 2Apply vendor patches for SimpleHelp (CVE-2024-57726 and companions) and Samsung MagicINFO 9 Server immediately; if patching is not possible, take the management interface off the public internet behind a VPN or allowlist.
  • 3For D-Link DIR-823X routers where no fix is yet available, disable remote/WAN administration, rotate admin credentials, and consider replacing with a supported model.
  • 4Review SimpleHelp technician accounts, sessions, and audit logs since January 2025 for unexpected admin role grants, new technicians, or unattended-access deployments.
  • 5Confirm BOD 22-01 remediation tickets are open with the May 2026 KEV deadline for any federal civilian systems.
● Strategic This week & beyond
  • 1Wire the CISA KEV feed directly into vulnerability management and ticketing pipelines so KEV additions auto-create high-priority remediation tickets with the BOD 22-01 SLA.
  • 2Segment non-IT-managed appliances (digital signage servers, building systems, SOHO routers) onto restricted VLANs with egress filtering so a compromise cannot pivot into core enterprise networks.
  • 3For MSPs and MSP customers, contractually require that remote-support tooling (SimpleHelp, ConnectWise, etc.) be patched within KEV deadlines and instrumented with EDR and session logging.
  • 4Replace end-of-life or thinly-supported edge devices (such as consumer-grade routers in business roles) with vendor-supported equipment that receives timely firmware updates.
  • 5Establish a quarterly attack-surface review specifically targeting 'shadow IT' management consoles — signage, KVM, OOB management, remote-support — that rarely show up in standard scans.
📚Concept BriefingClick a tag to expand
CISA KEV Catalog
A CISA-maintained list of CVEs with confirmed in-the-wild exploitation, governed by Binding Operational Directive 22-01.
This advisory covers the four CVEs added to KEV on April 25, 2026, with a May 2026 federal remediation deadline.
Binding Operational Directive 22-01
A 2021 CISA directive requiring federal civilian agencies to remediate KEV-listed vulnerabilities within set deadlines, typically two to three weeks.
BOD 22-01 is what turns the KEV listing into a legal patching obligation for federal agencies and a de facto SLA for many private-sector teams.
MSP Supply-Chain Risk
The risk that compromise of a managed service provider's tooling cascades into every downstream customer the MSP administers.
SimpleHelp is heavily used by MSPs, so CVE-2024-57726 is a one-to-many pivot point into thousands of client networks.
Edge Device Visibility Gap
The tendency for routers, signage controllers, and similar appliances to fall outside standard EDR, asset, and patch programs.
MagicINFO 9 and DIR-823X exemplify devices that often have no agent, no patch cadence, and no log shipping — exactly where active exploitation is now landing.
🔍Threat Hunting Signals
High Unexpected SimpleHelp technician account creation or role elevation ►
Look for newly created technician accounts, administrative role grants, or unattended-access agent deployments in SimpleHelp audit logs that do not match a change ticket. CVE-2024-57726 enables unauthorized administrative actions, so anomalous admin events are a direct exploitation indicator.

Medium Outbound connections from MagicINFO 9 Server to non-Samsung infrastructure ►
MagicINFO servers should primarily talk to Samsung update infrastructure and internal signage endpoints. Egress to unfamiliar IPs, especially over non-standard ports, suggests post-exploitation command-and-control.

Compromised SOHO routers commonly beacon to attacker infrastructure or pull additional payloads. Hunt for DNS/HTTP traffic sourced from the router's own management IP rather than client devices behind it.
